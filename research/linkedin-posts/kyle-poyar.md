### Post: Email Nurture Guide — What GOOD & GREAT Email Looks Like in B2B SaaS

- Date: January 2, 2024
- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-poyar_marketing-email-saas-activity-7148324794025877505-Akeg
- Topic: B2B SaaS email nurture frameworks, lifecycle email strategy
- Summary:
Kyle argues that email is the most powerful yet under-discussed channel for B2B SaaS growth. He contrasts what "GOOD" email looks like versus what "GREAT" email looks like, providing specific examples from companies like Canva, Deputy, Ring, Airtable, and Snyk. He also introduces the concept of "SaaS entropy" — the tendency for email programs to degrade over time as different teams add conflicting messages.

- Key ideas:
Email is cheap and works time and again, but it's rarely a 2024 resolution because it's "not exciting"
GOOD email includes: welcome email, personal founder intro, product adoption emails, upgrade email, bridge to GTM team, and product activity report
GREAT email adds: personalization to specific industries/use cases, trigger emails based on website activity (not just product actions), earning trust through flawless execution (no broken links, correct landing pages)
Optimize email based on outcomes, not vanity metrics like click-through rates or opens
"SaaS entropy" happens when product, marketing, sales, and growth teams all add to the email program without coordination — the result is a degraded user experience
Specific examples mentioned: Canva for Teams (welcome email), Mott & Bow (founder intro), Deputy (product adoption), Ring (upgrade email), Airtable (bridge to GTM), Snyk (product activity report)
Why this matters for B2B SaaS email / newsletter research:
This is the most practical email nurture framework available from a PLG expert. The GOOD vs. GREAT distinction provides an immediate audit tool for existing email programs. The "SaaS entropy" concept explains why email performance degrades over time even when individual tactics improve. The emphasis on outcome-based optimization (vs. open/click rates) is a critical corrective for teams chasing the wrong metrics. The specific company examples give concrete templates to adapt.

- Key quote: "This isn't new. It's not exciting. It's not on your 2024 resolution list. But it's cheap and it works, time and again. I'm talking about EMAIL. It's the most powerful channel for growth that we almost avoid talking about."
