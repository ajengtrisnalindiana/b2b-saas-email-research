### Post: Alex Hormozi's 3-7-30 Framework for Email Nurture

- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nathan-may1_alex-hormozi-revealed-the-3-7-30-framework-activity-7291455375046823936-1ZXU
- Topic: Email nurture sequence framework, education-first email marketing
- Summary:
Nathan May breaks down Alex Hormozi's 3-7-30 email nurture framework—the exact system used to drive 7-figure sales from a newsletter audience. The framework replaces traditional "sales-y" email nurture with an 80% education, 20% sales approach that builds trust before asking for the sale.

- Key ideas:
First 3 Days (Build Credibility):
Day 1: Best case study (client introduction → problem → solution → results with numbers)
Day 2: Best client transformation (before/after story, position your product as the bridge)
Day 3: Biggest industry insight (trends, hidden opportunities, insider tips)

Next 7 Days (Tackle Objections):
Email subscribers directly to uncover fears/concerns
Address objections individually: "Too expensive" (show 10X ROI case study), "Not for my industry" (feature wins across 5 niches), "No time to implement" (6-week accelerator)

Next 30 Days (Build Trust):
Send 2 emails per week
Alternate between success stories (more proof) and industry insights (more credibility)
Make emails valuable enough that prospects thank you even if they don't buy
Results for a $47 product using this framework: 46% open rate, 3.4% CTR, 23% sales page conversion, 7% upsell to $5K accelerator

- Why this matters for B2B SaaS email / newsletter research:
This framework solves the core problem of B2B email marketing: how to nurture without being "sales-y." The structured 40-day approach gives SaaS founders a ready-to-implement sequence. The 46% open rate and 23% conversion on a $47 product prove that education-first nurture works. For high-ticket B2B SaaS, adapting this framework can dramatically improve email-driven revenue. The 80/20 education-to-sales ratio provides a clear editorial guideline for any B2B newsletter.

- Key quote: *"The 3-7-30 framework is how you nurture without being sales-y. 80% education, 20% sales. People buy when they trust you."*
