### Post 1: The Decline of Cold Email — Why Most Outbound Has Become Spam

- Date: June 15, 2024
- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/orengreenberg_listen-stuart-i-get-youre-just-doing-your-activity-7207671464706064384-JVHc
- Topic: Cold email effectiveness, outbound personalization, B2B sales development
- Summary:
Oren shares a real interaction with an SDR who repeatedly ignored his request to communicate via email instead of phone calls. He argues that the vast majority of outbound outreach has become spam due to lack of research and personalization. Successful outbound efforts use a "sniper rather than machine-gun approach" — hyper-relevant, well-researched, and respectful of prospect preferences.

- Key ideas:
The vast majority of outbound is of such poor quality that it's become spam — it isn't the data vendors' fault
Successful outbound efforts prioritize research and personalization — sniper rather than machine-gun approach
I have bought services from cold outbound in the past, but it's closer to 1 in 1,000 emails/LinkedIn messages
Outbound is executed well only 1 in 250 times (and that's generous) — this is why it's getting harder to crack as a channel
CXOs can ask AI in call recording software (like Gong): "Show me transcripts where people complain about being called too often" and "What percentage of calls fit that criteria?" — this 5-10 minute action can save or make millions
Spending a fortune on marketing to create brand awareness while tarnishing your brand with poor quality outbound is madness

- Why this matters for B2B SaaS email / newsletter research:
Oren provides a clear diagnostic for why cold email fails for most B2B SaaS companies — lack of genuine personalization and respect for prospect preferences. The "sniper vs. machine-gun" framework is critical for any SaaS founder building an outbound email motion. The specific recommendation to use AI call recording software to audit sales outreach is a practical, actionable step most teams overlook. The 1-in-250 execution rate benchmark sets a realistic bar for quality.

- Key quote: "What successful outbound efforts do differently is prioritise research and personalisation. They make sure it's hyper-relevant. Sniper rather than machine-gun approach."

### Post 2: Why Marketing "Fails" in Niche B2B SaaS — The ABM Alternative

- Date: August 20, 2024
- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/orengreenberg_a-typical-conversation-highlighting-the-complexity-activity-7231917969927213057-IO8K
- Topic: Niche B2B marketing challenges, Account-Based Marketing (ABM), realistic marketing expectations

- Summary:
Oren addresses a common conversation with B2B SaaS founders who have spent money on LinkedIn ads, Google ads, and events but saw no results. He explains that for niche propositions with target audiences as small as 500-10,000 people worldwide (e.g., family offices, enterprise CFOs), traditional paid advertising inevitably creates massive wastage. The only viable alternative is Account-Based Marketing — focusing on a set list of companies and decision-makers with personalized, well-researched outreach.

- Key ideas:
Niche propositions with small target audiences (500-10,000 people worldwide) or tricky audiences (family offices, enterprise CFOs) make traditional paid ads inefficient
Marketing casting a wide net necessitates massive wastage — partly due to privacy, partly due to how search and social platforms monetize
If churn exceeds new customer acquisition, you need an alternative to grow
The only viable option for niche B2B comes down to Account Based Marketing — focusing on a set list of companies and decision makers with personalized, well-researched approach
Marketing will increase the probability of being seen, accelerating other sales activities — but if the expectation is that marketing will directly generate highly relevant ready-to-buy prospects in a short time frame for high ACV deals, you're likely to be disappointed

- Why this matters for B2B SaaS email / newsletter research:
This post directly informs email strategy for niche B2B SaaS companies. Oren argues that ABM — which relies heavily on personalized email and LinkedIn outreach — is the only viable channel when your TAM is tiny. The insight that "marketing casts a wide net where only a handful of relevant buyers get through" explains why spray-and-pray email fails for niche players. For newsletter operators, this reinforces that list quality (reaching the right 500 people) matters more than list size.

- Key quote: "If the expectation is that marketing will directly generate highly relevant ready-to-buy prospects in a short time frame that's easily measurable for high annual contract value deals — then you're likely to be disappointed."

### Post 3: 9-Step Approach to Help Tech Businesses Grow

- Date: July 27, 2023
- Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/orengreenberg_marketing-growth-techbusinesses-activity-7090590163180863488-7GXZ
- Topic: Growth marketing framework, paid media, user acquisition
- Summary:
Oren shares his agency's 9-step process for scaling tech businesses in 2-6 months, covering problem validation, persona definition, positioning, offer creation, landing page optimization, creative sourcing, paid ads scaling, performance optimization, and onboarding. The framework emphasizes high-velocity testing and retention-focused optimization.

- Key ideas:
Step 1: Go deep into the problem + market context — develop an innovative solution that people care about
Step 2: Define key persona(s) — use your audience's words, not "brand language," through customer interviews
Step 3: Positioning + messaging = compelling story — identify a "villain" and weave a relatable story
Step 4: Compelling offer — remember your offer is the 1st step into your funnel; freemium models work best
Step 5: Compelling landing page — social proof can have a 270% conversion uplift
Step 6: Source high-volume creatives — user-generated content and influencers
Step 7: Scale via paid ads — amplify best-performing organic content
Step 8: Optimize ads performance — aim for 100-150 new creatives a month
Step 9: Optimize onboarding/user journey — segment users, A/B test, focus on retention not just acquisition

- Why this matters for B2B SaaS email / newsletter research:
While focused on paid acquisition, Step 9 (onboarding optimization) directly relates to email nurture sequences. The emphasis on using the audience's words (not brand language) is critical for email copywriting — emails that sound like how customers actually talk outperform polished marketing speak. The 270% conversion uplift from social proof is a benchmark for including testimonials and case studies in email campaigns.

- Key quote: "Use your audience's words, not 'brand language.' Interview existing customers, using qualitative & quantitative analysis."
