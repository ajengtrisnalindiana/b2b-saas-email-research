# Nick Franklin — ChartMogul

## Resource
- **Title:** Fast Forever — How to Keep Shipping at Speed as Your SaaS Company Grows
- **Source:** ChartMogul Blog (adapted from ARRtist Summit talk)
- **Link:** https://chartmogul.com/blog/fast-forever-how-to-keep-shipping-at-speed-as-your-saas-company-grows

##Summary
Nick Franklin shares ChartMogul's 11-year journey of having product velocity, losing it, and then getting it back. Using number of commits as a proxy for "rate of innovation," he argues that fast shipping velocity is not just for startups—it can be sustained as companies grow. The post emphasizes transparency about the challenges of maintaining speed and offers practical lessons for founders facing similar friction as their teams scale.

##Why this matters for B2B SaaS email strategy
While this post focuses on product development velocity, the underlying principle directly applies to email program velocity. Many B2B SaaS companies start with high email cadence and personalization but lose it as they scale (more teams, more compliance, more stakeholders). Nick's framework of "losing it and getting it back" parallels the "SaaS entropy" concept in email—programs degrade over time unless intentionally rebuilt. For newsletter operators specifically, this speaks to maintaining consistent publishing velocity. The use of "number of commits" as a proxy for innovation is a useful mental model for tracking email program health (e.g., number of email variants tested, number of segmentation updates, number of personalization touches per campaign).
