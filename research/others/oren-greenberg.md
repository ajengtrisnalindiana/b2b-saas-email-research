## Summary
Oren Greenberg offers a free 6-part email diagnostic series for B2B SaaS leaders who have built successful businesses ($5-15M ARR) through sales or referrals but need marketing to scale. The series covers why marketing engines break and how to fix them. Additional posts cover topics like: what makes a compelling offer (value proposition + pricing + risk reversal), which channel to pick first, personal branding for SaaS CEOs, and positioning frameworks. The newsletter itself is a case study in B2B email marketing — Oren uses email to deliver high-value diagnostic content that attracts his ideal client (SaaS leaders at $5-15M ARR).

## Why this matters for B2B SaaS email strategy
This newsletter series is a masterclass in using email as a lead generation channel for high-ticket B2B services. The framing "your marketing keeps failing and it's probably not your strategy" is a powerful example of email subject line psychology that addresses a specific pain point. Key topics directly relevant to SaaS email strategy include:

The Offer framework — what separates good positioning from a good offer (value proposition + what customer gives up + risk reversal). This applies directly to email lead magnets and newsletter value propositions.

Channel selection insight — "which channel will deliver max leads in most cost-effective way?" is the wrong question; order of sequence matters. For email, this means understanding where email fits in your acquisition funnel relative to other channels.

Personal branding for SaaS CEOs — not every CEO should build a personal brand, but those with the right ingredients are missing a golden opportunity. This directly informs founder-led newsletter strategy.

"Features are not your friend" — marketing benefits, not features. This is a critical email copywriting principle for B2B SaaS.

Positioning ingredients — industry changes, the stakes, the enemy, customer problems, the promised land. These are the building blocks of effective email narrative.

For B2B SaaS founders and marketers, this resource provides both strategic frameworks for thinking about email's role in growth and tactical examples of how to structure an email course as a lead generation engine.
