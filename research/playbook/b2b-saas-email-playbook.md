## Playbook Outline

## Cold Email Outbound for Early-Stage B2B SaaS

## Section 1 - Scope & Assumptions

### What this playbook is for

- Early-stage B2B SaaS (pre-PMF to early PMF)
- Founder-led or very small sales teams
- Limited brand awareness
- Need for near-term customer acquisition, not long-term brand only

### What this playbook is NOT for

- Enterprise sales cycles
- Strong inbound/content engines already in place
- Highly regulated industries

## Section 2 - Core Philosophy: Why Cold Email Can Work at Early Stage

### Introduce the central tension:

- Early-stage founders face a tradeoff:
Build trust slowly via content or create demand immediately via direct outreach.

### Briefly introduce the two poles:

- Content-first (trust before pitch)
- Direct-response (value before trust)

## Section 3 — Cold Email Outbound SOP (Early-Stage B2B SaaS)

This SOP describes how an early-stage B2B SaaS team should execute cold email outbound, step by step, based on expert guidance and practical constraints (small team, limited brand, need for fast signal).

### 3.1 When to Use Cold Email (Qualification Gate)

Use cold email outbound if ALL of the following are true:
- You are pre-PMF or early PMF
- You have no meaningful inbound audience

You can clearly describe:
- A specific buyer (role + company type)
- A painful, expensive problem
- A measurable outcome

Rationale
Cold email allows founders to create demand and collect learning faster than content-driven approaches at the earliest stage.

Sources
 - Alex Hormozi — outcome-first demand creation (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/abbas-somji_alex-hormozi-uses-this-cold-email-framework-activity-7282319902789570560-ZyqY/, 06.01.2025)
- Jason Lemkin — “Outbound done well works” (https://www.saastr.com/10-things-that-always-work-in-marketing-with-saastr-founder-and-ceo-jason-lemkin-part-2-pod-653-video/, 20.04.2023)
- Lenny Rachitsky — cold email as early customer acquisition (https://www.focal.vc/founder-resources/cold-outbound-is-under-appreciated, 28.01.2025)

### 3.2 Define the ICP (Narrow > Broad)

Action
- Define ICP using role, company size, and core pain
- Avoid “anyone who could use this”

Example
Head of RevOps at B2B SaaS companies with 10–50 employees struggling with pipeline predictability

Rationale
Broad targeting dilutes relevance and reduces reply rates.

Sources
- Lemkin — narrow targeting for outbound efficiency (https://www.saastr.com/10-things-that-always-work-in-marketing-with-saastr-founder-and-ceo-jason-lemkin-part-2-pod-653-video/, 20.04.2023)
- Rachitsky — focus on early adopter profiles (https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/t/b2b, 29.08.2023)

### 3.3 Craft the Value Proposition (Outcome > Features)

Rule
- Do NOT describe your product
- Describe the outcome the buyer wants

Formula (Hormozi-style)

We help [ICP] achieve [Outcome] without [Pain / Tradeoff]

Example
We help early-stage SaaS founders book sales calls without hiring SDRs or running ads.

Rationale
Prospects care about results, not features or technology.

Sources
- Hormozi — Massive Value Proposition framework (https://metacast.app/podcast/the-game-with-alex-hormozi/Nhx5RXdz/unscalable-value-scalable-growth-ep-766/RK3heASP, 01.11.2024)
- Lemkin — buyer-outcome framing in outbound (https://www.saastr.com/does-outbound-still-work/, 07.05.2024)

### 3.4 Build the Lead List (Quality Control)

Actions :
- Build lists manually or semi-manually
- Validate:
- Role relevance
- Company stage
- Public signal of the problem (job post, growth signal, tech stack)

Avoid :
- Purchased mass lists
- Generic “CEO/Founder” blasts

Rationale
Early-stage outbound fails more from bad targeting than bad copy.

Source
Lemkin — outbound fails when targeting is lazy (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasonmlemkin_so-i-think-ai-will-destroy-outbound-what-activity-7228860303956918273-vV0-/, 12.08.2024)

### 3.5 Write the Cold Email (Plain, Direct, Short)

Format :
- Plain text only
- 50–120 words
- No images, no HTML, no attachments

Structure
- Context (why them)
- Problem recognition
- Outcome-oriented value
- Soft CTA

Example CTA
- Worth a short conversation?
- Should I send more details?

Rationale
Cold emails should feel like a personal note, not a campaign.

Sources
- Hormozi — clarity beats cleverness (https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/mastering-your-behavior-the-ultimate-game-changer-w/id1254720112?i=1000637249915, 02.12.2023)
- Lemkin — plain-text outbound best practices (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasonmlemkin_does-outbound-still-work-its-funny-two-activity-7186823302806437888-nkCE/, 17.04.2024)

### 3.6 Follow-Up Sequence (Polite Persistence)

Recommendations :
- 3–4 total emails
- 2–3 days between messages

Follow-Up Rules
- Do not say “just following up”
- Reframe value or angle each time
- Stop after final attempt

Rationale
Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email.

Sources
- Hormozi — structured follow-up systems (https://nowbam.com/violence-volume-zero-excuses-alex-hormozis-content-blueprint-for-agents/ 24.04.2025)
- Lemkin — persistence without spam (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/johnnypage_alex-hormozis-closer-framework-activity-7318416526204628992-ivwz/, 16.04.2025)

### 3.7 Volume & Scaling (Start Small)

Guidelines :
- Start with 10–20 emails/day
- Validate messaging before scaling
- Increase volume only after replies convert to calls

Rationale
Scaling broken messaging amplifies failure.

Sources
Lemkin — outbound discipline before scale (https://www.saastr.com/jason-lemkin-saastr-annual-closing-ama-part-one/, 13.12.2024)

### 3.8 Metrics to Track (Learning > Vanity)

Primary Metrics :
- Reply rate
- Positive reply rate
- Calls booked
- Deals influenced

De-prioritize :
- Open rates
- Click rates

Rationale
Early-stage outbound is about learning and conversations, not dashboard optics.

Sources
 OpenView Partners — revenue-aligned outbound metrics (https://openviewpartners.com/blog/saas-metrics-the-one-saas-metric-that-matters-most/, 04.04.2013)

### 3.9 Exit Criteria (When to Stop or Adapt)

Pause or rethink cold outbound if:
- <3% reply rate after message iteration
- Repeated objections indicate weak problem
- Leads do not convert to real conversations

Rationale
Cold email is a signal generator, not a magic trick.

Sources
Lemkin — outbound as validation tool (https://www.saastr.com/10-things-that-always-work-in-marketing-with-saastr-founder-and-ceo-jason-lemkin-part-2-pod-653-video/, 20.04.2023)

Why this SOP fits early-stage B2B SaaS
- Optimized for speed of learning
- Assumes low brand trust
- Minimizes operational overhead
- Maximizes signal per email sent

## Section 4 - Where Experts Disagree

### Disagreement 1: Cold Email vs Content-First Trust

**Author A: Dave Gerhardt**
Recommends building audience and trust before selling
Believes cold email is transactional and avoidable

**Author B: Alex Hormozi**
Advocates direct cold email with strong value framing
Believes demand can be created without prior trust

**My Position**
I side with Hormozi for early-stage SaaS
Reason: founders lack time, audience, and runway; cold email provides faster signal and learning

### Disagreement 2: Founder-Led Warmth vs Scaled Outbound

**Author A: Nathan May**
Prefers informal, founder-written newsletters and warm intros

**Author B: Lemkin / Hormozi**
Support scalable outbound systems

**My Position**
Warm approaches are powerful but not always available
Cold outbound is more reliable when networks are limited

### Disagreement 3: Email as Acquisition vs Nurture Channel

**Author A: Kyle Poyar**
Emphasizes email for onboarding, retention, and expansion

**Author B: Hormozi / Lenny**
Emphasize email for first contact and acquisition

**My Position**
Acquisition-first is more urgent pre-PMF
Retention strategies matter later

## Section 5 - What I Rejected and Why
**Rejected Idea 1: Content-Only Growth at Early Stage**
- Source: Gerhardt
- Reason for rejection:
- Requires time, consistency, and distribution
- Poor fit for founders needing fast feedback

**Rejected Idea 2: Heavy HTML & Brand-Polished Cold Emails**
- Source: general outbound advice
- Reason:
- Hurts deliverability
- Feels promotional and low-trust

## Section 6 - My Original Ideas
- Hybrid “Proof-on-Demand” Cold Email
- Cold email focused on outcome
- Link to one highly relevant proof asset (case snippet, teardown, Loom)
- Bridges trust gap without building full content engine

Why it could work:
- Faster than content marketing
- Stronger than pure cold pitch
- Reduces skepticism

## Section 7 - Weaknesses of This Playbook
Be explicit:
- Assumes clear ICP and painful problem
- Cold email fatigue may reduce response rates
- Less effective in crowded or spam-heavy niches
- Based largely on founder/operator perspectives, not academic data

## Section 9 - Final Positioning Statement

Cold email is not a replacement for trust —
It is a temporary substitute when trust has not yet been earned and learning speed matters most.
