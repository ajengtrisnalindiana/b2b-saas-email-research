## Why this video is included
This episode was selected because it provides tactical, real-world email lifecycle marketing strategies specifically for B2B SaaS teams. Featuring Charlotte Hardin (Rebuy Engine) and Naomi West (Customer.io), the episode covers high-performing welcome sequences, experimentation with small audiences, behavior-based triggers, plain text vs. designed emails, and navigating multi-year sales cycles. This is directly applicable to B2B SaaS founders and marketers building email-driven growth engines.

Why this matters for B2B SaaS email strategy
This episode is Dave Gerhardt's most directly relevant email content for B2B SaaS. Key takeaways for practitioners:

Welcome sequences are the highest-leverage email touchpoint — The insight that "welcome flows are always worth revamping" is a specific action item. Since welcome emails have the highest open rates, even small improvements yield significant ROI.

Testing with small audiences requires different methods — For niche B2B SaaS (TAM of 500-10,000 prospects), standard A/B testing (requiring thousands of users) isn't feasible. The episode offers alternative approaches to gain learnings without statistical significance.

Lifecycle content for long sales cycles — Multi-year enterprise sales require different email content than transactional or low-ticket B2B. The episode addresses how to keep leads engaged across extended buying windows.

Plain text often outperforms designed emails in B2B — This aligns with the broader industry trend: B2B buyers respond better to personal, conversational emails than polished marketing templates.

Benchmarks are misleading — Instead of comparing to industry averages, the episode advises tracking your own trends and focusing on outcomes (conversions, pipeline) rather than vanity metrics (opens, clicks).
